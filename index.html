Research Interests

  • Model Interpretability: My key interests lie in demystifying the "black box" nature of AI models. I focus on input feature analysis, exploring architectural modifications, and developing techniques to better explain and visualize model decision-making processes.
  • Multi-Agent AI Systems: I enjoy designing and implementing multi-agent systems to tackle complex tasks such as reasoning, task automation, and job scheduling. My work involves optimizing interactions between agents to enhance collaborative problem-solving and decision-making.
  • Retrieval Methods (RAGs) for LLMs: I am particularly interested in areas like Knowledge Graphs, GraphRAGs, and the engineering of RAG-based systems. My focus is on improving the retrieval mechanisms in language models to enhance their knowledge integration and contextual understanding.
  • Datasets and Benchmarking: My previous work includes generating synthetic datasets, minimizing hallucinations in language models, curating complex datasets, and developing benchmark metrics to evaluate model performance and reliability.

Past Projects

  1. Speech Aya
    I worked on an 8-week long Expedition Aya where I accomplished the following:
    • Collected spoken data for the concerned languages (including both speech-only data and some ASR data) to enable the LLM to map text tokens to speech tokens and transfer text knowledge to the speech modality.
    • Discretized the spoken data using existing models (MMS, mHuBERT, XEUS, etc.) and methods (k-Means, RepCoder, SpeechTokenizer, etc.).
    • Continued pretraining an Aya model on these tokens.
    • Performed instruction fine-tuning, enabling the model to execute speech tasks (ASR, TTS, voice cloning, Speech2Speech translation, etc.) when prompted.
    • Developed a method for speech synthesis to generate high-quality speech from the speech tokens, utilizing techniques like coupling SoundStream, EncoDec, and diffusion-based speech synthesis.
  2. Audio Handling and Music Classification
